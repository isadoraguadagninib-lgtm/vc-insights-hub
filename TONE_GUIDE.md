# Kiara Capital — Editorial Voice & Tone Guide

This guide defines how every article on Kiara Insights should read. It is calibrated from Kiara's LP Letters (Q3 2025 – Q1 2026) and Michael Esrubilsky's LinkedIn posts. Any human editor or automated agent writing for this blog must follow it.

---

## The Kiara Voice in One Sentence

**Direct. Analytical. Contrarian. Always connected to a thesis.**

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Take a stance
Every article has a point of view. We do not write "on one hand / on the other hand" pieces. We say what we think, back it with evidence, and let the reader disagree if they want.

> ✅ "AI isn't a vertical. It's the horizontal layer transforming every industry."
> ❌ "AI is having a significant impact across many sectors and it's worth considering both sides."

### 2. Be specific
Reference real companies, funds, regulations, founders, and data points. Generic observations are noise. Specific ones are signal.

> ✅ "OpenAI's $300B valuation following its largest-ever private fundraise is not a story about AI hype — it's a story about infrastructure becoming monopolistic."
> ❌ "Major AI companies are reaching unprecedented valuations, showing the sector's growth."

### 3. Find the angle others aren't covering
Start from what is overlooked or misunderstood. The consensus view is rarely worth writing about.

> ✅ "Everyone wants to talk about card readers. The real opportunity lies in tailor-made payment solutions for mid-sized businesses."
> ❌ "Fintech continues to grow rapidly as more companies adopt digital payment solutions."

### 4. Connect to structural shifts
Today's news is only worth writing about if it points to something durable. Ask: what does this mean for the next 3–5 years?

### 5. Never manufacture urgency
We do not chase headlines or write about things that don't matter. If there is no meaningful insight, we don't write the piece.

> *"Sharing platitudes or soft news is not really our style."* — Kiara Q4 2025 LP Letter

## Structure

### Opening (the hook)
The first sentence must earn the reader's attention. Use one of these approaches:
- A counter-intuitive observation
- A sharp, specific question
- A bold claim that needs unpacking
- A striking data point

**Never open with:** "In today's world...", "As AI continues to...", "It is no secret that..."

### Body
- Short paragraphs: 2–4 sentences maximum
- No bullet-point listicles — prefer analytical prose
- Each paragraph should advance the argument, not repeat it
- Use real names, real numbers, real events

### Closing
End with a reflection, a tension, or a forward-looking question. Not a CTA. Not a summary.

> ✅ "The window is open. But windows don't stay open forever."
> ❌ "In conclusion, AI presents exciting opportunities for investors who are willing to act."

## Language

### Words to avoid
| Banned | Why |
|---|---|
| game-changer | Empty |
| landscape | Overused, vague |
| delve into | AI cliché |
| leverage (verb) | Corporate jargon |
| cutting-edge | Hollow |
| robust | Meaningless filler |
| seamless | Meaningless filler |
| it's worth noting | Hedge |
| in conclusion | Lazy |
| revolutionary | Almost always untrue |
| disruptive | Overused |

### Words that fit the voice
`conviction` · `signal` · `noise` · `thesis` · `structural` · `agentic` · `moat` · `traction` · `data layer` · `application layer` · `founder-centric` · `discipline` · `clarity` · `pattern` · `inflection point`

## Kiara's Investment Thesis (always relevant)

Articles should connect — explicitly or implicitly — to one or more of these themes:

**AI at the application and data layer**
Not infrastructure, not LLMs. The companies worth backing are those applying AI on top of defensible data and workflows within financial services.

**Agentic finance**
AI is moving from being a feature inside products to being the layer products run on. Credit, fraud, compliance, wealth management — all being rebuilt from scratch.

**Data moats**
Proprietary, siloed data becomes a defensible moat for superior financial decisions. This is where durable competitive advantages are built.

**Intelligent workflows**
AI transforming SMB ERPs, accounting platforms, fiduciary services, KYC/identity. The opportunity is in broken workflows with massive markets.

**Accessible finance**
Making complex or historically expensive processes scalable. Tax, accounting, compliance, fiduciary services — the long tail of finance.

**Brazil and Latin America as structural opportunity**
Not because it's easy — but because necessity combined with creativity produces innovation. Emerging markets do not wait for the playbook.

**Founder-centric investing**
Only exceptional teams win. AI represents a new frontier, and top-tier talent is scarce outside Silicon Valley. Backing the right people matters more than backing the right category.

**Signal vs. noise**
In a world flooded with AI hype, discipline is a competitive advantage. We will not chase infrastructure or "picks and shovels" dominated by Silicon Valley giants.

**Conviction over consensus**
Those who wait for consensus often arrive too late. We back ideas before they become obvious.

## What We Cover

| Topic | What makes a good article |
|---|---|
| Venture Capital | Specific round, fund thesis, LP dynamics, Brazil/LatAm ecosystem |
| Artificial Intelligence | Agentic AI, enterprise adoption, model releases that shift the application layer |
| Fintech | Pix, Drex, Open Finance phases, neobank traction, payment infrastructure |
| Startups | Notable founding story, unusual distribution model, signal from early traction |
| Deep Tech | When it intersects with finance or emerging markets |
| Regulation | CVM, Banco Central, SEC rulings — implications for founders and investors |
| ESG & Impact | When returns and purpose are both provably present |

---

## What We Don't Cover

- Pure macro/politics with no investment angle
- Generic "AI is growing fast" pieces without a specific insight
- Company PR announcements without editorial interpretation
- Anything that requires us to take a position we can't defend with evidence
